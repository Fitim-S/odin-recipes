Lasagna

lasagna background

lasagna, pasta dish of Italian origin, made with broad often ruffled noodles and a tomato or white sauce. Lasagna, in the singular, is a southern Italian variation of what northern Italians call lasagne, in the plural.

Ingredients

  • Olive oil
  • 500g beef mince
  • Passata
  • Lasagna sheets
  • White sauce
  • 125g Mozzarella

Steps

  1. To make the meat sauce, heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a frying pan and cook 750g lean beef mince in two batches for about 10 mins until browned all over.
  2. Finely chop 4 slices of prosciutto from a 90g pack, then stir through the meat mixture.
  3. Pour over 800g passata or half our basic tomato sauce recipe and 200ml hot beef stock. Add a little grated nutmeg, then season.
  4. Heat the oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4 and lightly oil an ovenproof dish
  5. Spoon one third of the meat sauce into the dish, then cover with some fresh lasagne sheets from a 300g pack. Drizzle over roughly 130g ready-made or homemade white sauce.
  6. Repeat until you have three layers of pasta. Cover with the remaining 390g white sauce, making sure you canâ€™t see any pasta poking through.
  7. Scatter 125g torn mozzarella over the top.
  8. Arrange the rest of the prosciutto on top. Bake for 45 mins until the top is bubbling and lightly browned.
